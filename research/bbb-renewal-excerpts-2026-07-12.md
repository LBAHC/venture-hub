# Thematic tally (40 excerpts)

| Theme | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rate shock at renewal (large unexplained increase, "lure you in" first-year pricing) | 19 | Heaviest at Branch, Slide, SageSure; Branch shows a distinct "teaser rate then triple at renewal" pattern (4 separate reviewers use lure/attract language) |
| Silence / no proactive contact before renewal or non-renewal | 8 | Several learned of increases from their mortgage company or bank statement |
| Auto-renewal / charged without consent or review | 5 | Square One (×2), Lemonade, Slide escrow draw, auto-switch |
| Poor follow-up or bait-and-switch on quotes | 4 | Insurify (×3), Flynn (promised to shop, went silent) |
| Positive: agency shopped/saved money or proactively reached out | 11 | Concentrated in the two small independent agencies (Atlantic, Flynn) |

**Cross-cutting observation for the data piece:** negative renewal themes cluster at carriers and insurtechs; nearly every "they shopped around for me / saved me $X" quote came from small independent agencies — *the independent service model wins when someone actually does the follow-up.* And the single worst independent-agency complaint in the set is precisely a follow-up failure ("said they would shop around... called and emailed numerous times with no response"). That gap — between what independents are positioned to do and what actually happens when nobody runs the process — IS our product thesis, in policyholders' own words.
