# AI-Slop Tells & High-Craft Landing Principles — research + self-audit
*Researched 2026-07-15 (sources: impeccable.style/slop, solodesign.cc tells list, Developers Digest 16-pattern audit, 925 Studios, tropes.fyi, Dead Language Society, DESIGN.md editorial-grid, Refero/Ramp system, Grid Rebels 2026 SaaS list, NN/g older-user research, Stanford Web Credibility.)*

## Key finding
The cream/editorial-serif/mono-accents aesthetic is now itself a **named second-generation slop cluster** ("the warm accent," "the safe green," "oversized italic serif hero"). After AI models got told to stop making purple-gradient sites, their defaults migrated to exactly our look. The palette isn't disqualified — Fraunces specifically is NOT on the slop lists — but execution must diverge everywhere else: **if the page is centered, symmetric, and eyebrow-labeled, cream+serif reads as slop; if it's set like an actual document, it reads as craft.**

## High-craft rules adopted (v3 redesign)
1. **Document, not template:** dominant text column + narrow mono-annotation margin column (editorial grid); margin notes replace eyebrow labels entirely.
2. **Vary section anatomy** — ruled ledger list, definition rows, plain price table, letter block: each section a different skeleton.
3. **Kill ornament:** no grain, no gradient, no offset shadows, no stamps, no numbers-as-decoration, no entrance animation. Hairlines and spacing do the work.
4. **Green = action only** (Ramp rule): CTAs and the money figures. Labels/annotations go ink.
5. **Scale contrast + readability floors for 45-65 audience:** 18px+ body, mono ≥14px and dark (NN/g: usability declines ~0.8%/yr from 25; AA contrast everywhere).
6. **Evidence over decoration:** the sample report artifact stays (it's the deliverable); the 5,408-review study gets cited on the landing page — real research is the one thing template sites can't fake (Stanford: 75% judge credibility from design; evidence-led = the trust signal).
7. **Copy hygiene:** strategic anti-positioning line stays (it's researched strategy, not a style tic); interior negation-pivot repeats and em-dash chains reduced.

## Rationale for what stayed
- Fraunces + Newsreader + Plex Mono: not on slop lists, roles are disciplined (voice/data).
- The wedge headline ("You don't need another tool...") — kept: it's the positioning derived from the competitor audit, not decoration.
- Sticky mobile CTA, price-in-button, visible pricing, week-by-week: conversion structure from the competitor audit, untouched.
