## AGENCYRETAIN SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is between **[AgencyRetain legal entity — TODO: Luke to confirm entity type/name]** ("Provider") and the agency identified in the signature block ("Client"), effective on the date of last signature.

### 1. Services
Provider will perform the service tier selected below:
- **☐ Renewal Recovery Sprint** — a 14-day engagement covering one segment of Client's book: book analysis, a Leakage Snapshot report, one email campaign (up to 3 touches) built in Client's name and voice, reply monitoring and producer routing, and a final report. Fee: **$500, one-time, due at signing.**
- **☐ Operator Retainer — Starter** — continuous operation of renewal and quote follow-up campaigns for one line of business, monthly performance report. Fee: **$1,000/month.**
- **☐ Operator Retainer — Full Book** — continuous operation of renewal, quote follow-up, and cross-sell campaigns across Client's book, monthly performance report. Fee: **$1,500/month.**

Retainers are month-to-month; either party may cancel with 15 days' written notice. No long-term commitment.

### 2. What Provider does not do
Provider is a marketing-operations service. Provider is not an insurance producer, does not sell, solicit, or negotiate insurance, does not give coverage advice, and does not communicate coverage terms to policyholders except content Client has approved. All campaign content is sent in Client's name; Client retains the producer relationship with every policyholder.

### 3. Client approval gate
No campaign launches without Client's written (including electronic) approval of the content, audience segment, and send schedule. Client approval covers accuracy of insurance-related statements; Provider covers execution, timing, and compliance mechanics.

### 4. Consent attestation (required)
Client attests that the policyholder contact information provided to Provider was collected by Client in the course of its business relationship with those policyholders, and that Client is not aware of any contact on the list who has opted out of communications from Client. Provider relies on this attestation. SMS, where used at all, is limited to informational policy/renewal notices to contacts for whom Client attests documented consent exists; cross-sell content is never sent by SMS.

### 5. Compliance mechanics (Provider obligations)
Provider will: (a) include Client's physical address and a functioning opt-out mechanism in campaign emails; (b) process opt-outs immediately upon receipt and maintain a per-client suppression list across all channels; (c) apply state-specific sending windows and frequency caps; (d) maintain a consent/opt-out audit log available to Client on request.

### 6. Data handling
Client data (book exports, policyholder contact information, campaign results) will be: stored in a per-client folder, access-limited to this engagement; never sold, shared, or used for any other client or purpose, including model training or marketing to policyholders on Provider's own behalf; deleted within 30 days of Client's written request or termination, except records legally required for the audit log. Provider will report any data incident affecting Client data within 72 hours of discovery.

### 7. Results
Provider does not guarantee specific retention rates, renewal counts, or revenue outcomes, and Client should disregard any projection as a promise. Sprint reports state actual measured results only. **Sprint risk-reversal:** if, in Client's reasonable judgment, the Sprint produced no measurable value, Client is under no obligation to continue to any retainer.

### 8. Liability cap
Provider's total liability under this Agreement is capped at the fees paid by Client in the three (3) months preceding the claim.
