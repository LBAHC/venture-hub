# LinkedIn Playbook — Week-1 Light Version
**Mode:** free tier, Luke's real profile, ~15 min/day. Claude drafts everything; Luke approves and clicks. No automation tools touch the account (ban risk on a real profile is unrecoverable). Upgrade path: Sales Navigator (~$100/mo) once replies justify it.

## Why this exists (the data)
Adding a LinkedIn touch around email sends is the cheapest documented multi-channel lift (1.5–3x reply rates). Engaged-first DMs reply at 15–25% vs 5–10% pure cold. Source: research/outreach-channels-2026-07-12.md.

## One-time setup (30 min, this week)
1. **Profile tune-up** (Claude drafts, Luke pastes): headline → "Founder, AgencyRetain — done-for-you renewal & follow-up operation for independent P&C agencies"; About section → the operator positioning (drafted below); banner → simple brand-color band (Claude generates).
2. Follow 20–30 niche voices (agency-owner community: Bradley Flowers, David Carothers, IAOA people, Big "I" chapters) so the feed surfaces engagement targets.

### About-section draft (honest — no invented credentials)
> Independent agencies keep buying retention software that nobody has time to run. AgencyRetain is the other half of that purchase: we operate your existing stack — quote follow-up, renewal outreach from 90 days out, cross-sell — and report results in dollars recovered, not open rates. New firm, deliberately small, priced to be tested: $500 gets you a 14-day sprint on one segment of your book. If it doesn't produce, we part friends.

## Daily routine (~15 min)
1. **10–15 connection requests** to agency owners/principals (list supplied by Claude from the lead pipeline — same ICP as email, DIFFERENT contacts until Sales Nav; never both channels on the same person in week 1).
2. **Accept-and-greet:** new acceptances get DM Touch 1 within 24h (drafts below — Luke may lightly personalize).
3. **2–3 thoughtful comments** on posts by niche voices (Claude suggests the posts + draft comments in the daily brief).

## Weekly: 2 posts (Claude drafts, Luke approves)
Rules: no fabricated stories, no invented clients. Content = public-benchmark math, honest new-firm build notes, and (once live) the calculator + review-analysis data piece.

**Post 1 draft — the math hook:**
> Every independent agency owner knows retention matters. Almost none have seen it as a number.
> Industry average retention: ~84%. Top-quartile independents: 93–95%.
> On a 2,000-policy book at $175 average commission, that 9-point gap is ~$31,500 walking out the door every year — policies you already won, lost to silence.
> The fix usually isn't new software. Most agencies already own a tool that could do renewal outreach. It's that nobody's running it.
> (Built a free calculator that does this math on your book size — link in comments. No email gate.)

**Post 2 draft — honest build-in-public:**
> We're a new firm doing an unfashionable thing: operating other people's software.
> Agencies buy Levitate or Fuse or AgencyZoom, use 20% of it, and the renewal leakage continues — not because the tool is bad, but because operating it is a job nobody in a 6-person agency has time for.
> So that's the whole business: we run your stack, you get a monthly report in dollars recovered. First engagement is $500, flat, 14 days, so nobody has to trust a new vendor on faith.
> Building it in the open here. Questions and skepticism welcome — especially skepticism.

## DM sequence (post-acceptance, 1–2 line, never a pitch-wall)
- **T1 (within 24h):** "Thanks for connecting, {first}. Quick honest question while I'm building in this space — does {agency} have anyone who actually runs renewal outreach on a schedule, or does it happen when someone gets a free hour? (Genuinely researching, not a pitch.)"
- **T2 (if reply, conversational):** respond genuinely to what they said; if pain surfaces → "That's exactly the gap we work on. We run a $500 flat 14-day test on one segment of a book — dollars-recovered report at the end. Worth a look? {calculator/landing link}"
- **T3 (no reply, +7 days):** share the calculator or data piece with zero ask: "No reply needed — built this free retention-math tool, thought of your book size. {link}"

## Kill/scale rules
- Acceptance rate <25% after 100 invites → tighten targeting before sending more.
- Any LinkedIn warning/restriction → full stop, report to Luke same day.
- 3+ positive DM conversations/week sustained → propose Sales Nav upgrade (real filters, 2.5x invite headroom).
