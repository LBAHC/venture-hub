## Post 1 — the study teaser (run after Luke approves the study)
We pulled 5,408 Google reviews across 211 independent insurance agencies to answer one question: what do policyholders actually praise?

It wasn't price. It wasn't the app. The single most common concrete theme in five-star reviews — by a wide margin — was some version of:

"They shopped the market for me and saved me money."

121 reviews said it almost word for word. 98% of them were 4-5 stars.

And the one-star reviews at those same agencies? Almost never about premiums. They're about silence: "never called me back," "promised a quote that never came," "the rate went up and nobody explained why."

Policyholders blame carriers for price. They credit — or blame — their agency for attention.

Full study with method and caveats in the comments. (Yes, we published the biases that cut against us too.)

## Post 2 — the renewal-timing math
The renewal call that wins happens 90 days out. Most agencies start at 30.

By 30 days, the shopping already happened — your policyholder saw the renewal number, got the mailer from the other agency, maybe already has a quote.

Industry average retention: ~84%. Top-quartile independents: 93-95%.
On a 2,000-policy book at $175 average commission, that gap is ~$31,500/year in commission walking out — from policies you already won.

The fix isn't software. Most agencies already own a tool that can schedule renewal outreach. The gap is that running it every week, on every policy, is a job — and nobody in a 6-person agency has that job.

## Post 3 — the best review we found (real quote, real agency, credited)
Found this in a five-star Google review of an Oklahoma City independent agency while doing research:

"Phu had reached to me last year asking to put together a quote for me. At that time, it wasn't much of a cost difference… but he asked if he could reach out after I received my renewal notices. He called again, and the new quotes he put together won the business."

Read what happened there. The producer lost the first quote — then diarized the renewal date, called back on it, and won the account.

To the customer this reads like magic. Operationally, it's a calendar entry.

Every agency knows this works. Almost none do it systematically, because someone has to run it. That's the entire gap between 84% retention and 94%.

## Post 4 — honest build-in-public
We're three weeks into building an unfashionable business: operating other people's software.

Independent agencies buy Levitate, or Fuse, or AgencyZoom — good tools — use 20% of them, and the renewal leakage continues. Not because the tool failed. Because operating it is a job, and nobody has time for it.

What surprised us in the research so far:
- The retention software market is crowded; the retention *operations* market is nearly empty
- Policyholder reviews almost never complain about price at independents — they complain about silence
- The agencies collecting 200+ Google reviews aren't lucky; review collection is a system, run weekly, like everything else that works

So that's the bet: we're the operators. First engagement is $500 flat so nobody has to trust a new firm on faith.

Skepticism welcome. It sharpens the model.
